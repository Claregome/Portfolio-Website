About Me

I didn’t get into tech because I had everything figured out. I got into it because I was curious.

I’ve always been the kind of person who wants to understand how things work especially the things we can’t see. How does a website respond instantly when you click a button? How does data travel across networks? How do systems stay secure when so many threats exist?

When I started studying Computer Science, that curiosity grew into something more intentional. I began exploring different areas cybersecurity, web development, system design and I realized I didn’t want to limit myself to just one side of technology.

What excites me is the balance. I enjoy the logical side of tech analyzing problems, understanding vulnerabilities, building secure systems, and thinking critically about how software is structured. There’s something satisfying about breaking down complex challenges and solving them step by step.

But I’m also deeply drawn to design. I notice spacing, color, layout, and flow. I care about whether something feels intuitive. I enjoy making interfaces clean, simple, and aesthetically pleasing. For me, a system shouldn’t just work it should feel good to use.

Through projects like deploying intrusion detection systems, building malware analysis environments, creating referral dashboards, and designing web applications, I’ve learned that great technology lives at the intersection of structure and creativity. Security and usability. Logic and design.

I’m still learning, still experimenting and still growing. And that’s what I love most about this field there’s always more to understand, more to build, and more to improve. My goal isn’t just to become a developer or a security professional. It’s to become someone who builds thoughtful, secure, and meaningful systems systems that are reliable behind the scenes and beautiful on the surface.

For me, a system shouldn’t just work it should feel good to use.
